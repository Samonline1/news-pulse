### main.py

> This is the entry point.

Flow:

```
RSS Feed

↓

Fetch Articles

↓

Extract Full Content

↓

Remove Duplicates

↓

Cluster Articles

↓

Store in MongoDB
```

---

### feeds/

Purpose:

* Fetch RSS feeds
* Normalize different feed formats
* Extract:

  * Title
  * Summary
  * Published Date
  * Source
  * Link
  * Full Content

---

### database/

Purpose:

Before inserting:

```
Already Exists?

↓

Yes → Skip

No → Insert
```

Keeps scraper rerunnable without duplicate articles.

---

### clustering/

Flow:

```
Article

↓

Lowercase Text

↓

Remove Stop Words

↓

Extract Keywords

↓

Compare Keyword Overlap

↓

Assign Cluster

↓

Generate Cluster Label
```

If enough meaningful keywords overlap, articles are grouped into the same cluster.

---

### Why Keyword Overlap?

> I chose keyword-overlap because the assignment recommends a simple, reliable approach over unnecessary complexity. It is deterministic, easy to explain, and worked well for clustering related news stories.

# ⚠️ 4. Challenge (25–30 sec)

Choose **one** challenge to stay within the time limit.

### Challenge

* Different RSS feeds use different formats
* Different date formats
* Missing fields
* Some article pages fail to parse

Solution:

* Normalize all feeds into one schema
* Add exception handling
* Continue processing even if one article fails

**OR**

Threshold challenge:

* Too low → unrelated articles merge
* Too high → related articles split
* Tuned the threshold until clusters became reasonably accurate

# 🚀 5. Future Improvement (10–15 sec)

> With more time, I would replace keyword-overlap clustering with semantic embeddings so articles discussing the same event using different wording could still be grouped together more accurately.
